Chiara Cascino

Art has always been present in my life, despite coming from a rational and pragmatic family where science prevailed. Since I was a child, I was taught the fundamentals of sports, painting, music, and literature… and little by little, I found my place. My way of expressing myself. I found my voice in dance. For me, dance reflects what opera represents for Wagner: the great total work of art. An extreme form of expression, fusion, and cohesion. An international language that, through its physicality, expresses millions of emotions and feelings. A passion that has become a profession, where the personal and professional are subtly intertwined.

Fun fact: You can find me either sending an email or with a glass of wine in my hand (or both).Love the sea and my doggies.

Fernando Gabriel Luis

I feel a lot, I think a lot and I move a lot. Dance is the place where all of these can merge as a whole, and where I am not too much or too little. Dance is where my deepest sense of soul can honestly thrive. I want to move an audience with that honesty so that they can feel freely in their way, question without judgment to themselves and be unlimitedly curious, as much as I am. Pushing what is stablished and inspiring whilst getting inspired. Giving and receiving, conversation, exchange; stryking to find a balance in this unstable present. I aim to find a balance through dance, within myself and with the audience, by being fearlessly extreme and simultaneously present in the now.

I’m interested in the human experience that binds our flesh and that can be guided into choreography.

Edit Domoszlai

Director of Gradient

As a dancer, she began her career in 2009 with the Pécs Ballet, where she worked with choreographers such as Balázs Vincze, Lóránd Zachár, Leo Mujic, and Cameron McMillan.
In 2014, she joined the Rambert Dance Company in London, where she performed in works by choreographers including Wayne McGregor, Sharon Eyal, and Kim Brandstrup.
From 2022, she worked as a freelancer, collaborating with the Israeli L-E-V company and also performing as a dancer with Opera Bonn.
As a choreographer, she performed at the 59th Venice Biennale, created a piece for the Dance Arts Faculty program in Rome, and also choreographed at the Hungarian University of Dance.
Between 2018 and 2022, she served as an ambassador for the Dancers Career Development organization.
In addition to her artistic work, she studied andragogy at the University of Pécs in 2012, continued her studies there with a master's degree, and earned an MA in Human Resources Consulting.
Later, in London, she obtained a CIPD Level 3 qualification in Learning and Development in 2017, and in 2023, she completed the 'MA Education and Technology' program at University College London.
In 2024, she joined the Csokonai National Theatre in Debrecen as the head of the dance department.

Fernando Gabriel Luis

Rehearsal Director

He was born and raised in Santa Úrsula, Tenerife. He began dancing at the local El Sauzal Ballet School and took a more serious step in 2010 under the supervision of Rosalina Ripoll and Miguel Navarro. As a professional dancer, he made his debut with the Tenerife Ballet, where he appeared in several lead roles. He then continued his career in Madrid, where he expanded his skills at the María de Ávila Superior Dance Conservatory and worked with choreographers such as Nacho Duato and Tony Fabre. In 2016, he joined the Dantzaz Company, performing in choreographies by Itzik Galili, Lukas Timulak, Jone San Martin, Christine Hassid, and Paolo Mohovich. Two years later, he moved to Hungary, joining the Székesfehérvár Ballet Theater, where he frequently appeared in lead roles, many of which were choreographed specifically to suit his style. He also had the opportunity to perform in works by Alexander Ekman and Jiri Kylian and began working as a ballet master and assistant choreographer in addition to his dance career. In 2023, he joined the dance division of the Csokonai National Theater in Debrecen.
